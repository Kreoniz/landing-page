Interesting facts about crustaceans:

Crustaceans are arthropods. Their skeletons are on the outside of their bodies.
Various species have occupied almost every conceivable niche within the aquatic environment.
The lifespan of a small crab averages around 3-4 years, but larger species such as the giant Japanese spider crab can live as long as 100 years.
The scientific study of crustaceans is known as carcinology and a scientist who works in carcinology is a carcinologist.
